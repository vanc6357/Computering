Inclined planes
Inclined planes:

When an object is on an inclined plane, its gravitational force is the only force acting on it without a tilted axis.

To facilitate calculations, the x and y axis are re-aligned upon the inclined plane. This way, all forces acting on the object (except for Fg) are aligned with the x or y axis.

Fg now has a new direction, and adopts an x and y component.
- The x component of Fg is responsible for the movement of the object down the incline. Formula: FgSinθ
- The y component of Fg is responsible for being equal and opposite in sign to the normal force of the object. Formula: FgCosθ
