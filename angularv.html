Angular Velocity
Angular velocity is the rate of change of theta over time.



ω = Δθ/Δt [rad/s]

ω > θ .....motion is counter clockwise
ω < θ .....motion is clockwise


Formula that will be useful in the future:


ω = 2πr/T [rad/s]

2πr = diameter of circle
T = time recquired to complete a full circle
Tangential velocity:

Definition: The velocity of the object in a direction perpendicular to the radius of the circle



Formula = Vt = r*ω


r = radius
ω = angular velocity

How the formula was attained:


1. Vt = arc length / Δt

2. Vt = 2πr * (θ/360) / Δt

3. 2π/360 gets cancelled, as it is equivalent to 1

4. Vt = r * θ / Δt

5. Vt = r*ω
