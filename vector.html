Vectors

There are two components to a vector:

1.Magnitude
2.Direction

Magnitude is the amount of units involved in a certain vector, while direction is the orientation of the vector.

Ex: a squirrel scampers two hundred meters east to hide his nuts behind a tree.

Magnitude is two hundred meters.
Direction is east.
Proper notation:[200m E]
Magnitude is not always a distance (meters). Magnitude can also be:

-force (N)
-velocity (m/s)
-acceleration (m/s2)
-etc...

Measuring a vector recquires the breaking down of its x and y component.

How to:
1. place the vector on a Cartesian plane
2. Use trigonometry to find the value of the x and y components
3. With the Pythagoras theorem, calculate the value of the hypotenuse, aka the magnitude of vector.
4. With inverse tan, calculate the angle of the hypotenuse to get the direction in degrees.
