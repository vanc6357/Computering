Centripetal force
Definition: Any force directed to a fixed center (a center-seeking force)



Formula: F= m* (v^2 / r)
m=mass of the object in motion
v= velocity of the object in motion(m/s)
r = radius of the circular trajectory

If the centripetal force acting on an object is disrupted, the object will then be released in a direction tangential to its path along the circle.



Main points:

- Velocity is always tangential to the circular path
- There is no change in velocity, but instead a constant change in direction, which causes an acceleration
- The force is going inwards, never outwards.
- The longer the radius, the less centripetal force required.
On a banked curve, the centripetal force is the horizontal component of the normal force. Fn will have to be broken down into an x and y vector.

The x component will be equal to the centripetal force acting on the object.
The y component will be equal to the force of gravity.
