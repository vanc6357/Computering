2D Kinematics
2D Kinematics:

The object's x and y trajectory must be combined in a single graph so that its trajectory can be depicted.
The graph can be broken down into two graphs to solve problems: x(t) and y(t), x and y being the axis of the object and t being time.



y(t) graph: Since acceleration of gravity applies to the y-axis, it must be plugged into the rule of the y(t) graph. As a result, the y(t) graph should look similar to y(x) graph.

X(t) graph: Since gravity goes along the y-axis, and wind resistance is ignored, there is no acceleration on the x-axis. As a result, the x(t) function continually increases.
