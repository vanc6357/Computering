Pulleys
Note: All pulleys are frictionless and air resistance is disregarded in pulley problems

When solving for a pulley problem, the acceleration of gravity can be acting on two masses. The force of gravity is always going to be responsible for the change in direction of a pulley.

Fg1 and fg2 are the only forces present. Therefore, there is no normal force, and instead, the forces of gravity provide a force of the opposite direction on each mass.




In a different pulley problem, there may be only one gravitational force acting on both masses.
The force of gravity acts as a force applied on one of the masses, as it is responsible for making it move forward.

If friction is added to the problem, the friction will simply be slowing the the acceleration of the force of gravity.
