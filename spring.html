Spring force
Definition: The force obtained by compressing or extending a spring
Formula: Fs= K*x

K is the spring constant (N/m)
- The spring constant is unique to every spring. The higher the K, the stiffer the spring.
X is the change in compression (m)
- If a spring is 12 cm when relaxed, and it is compressed until it is 8 cm, then the x will be equal to 4 cm.
Example problem:

A compressible spring has a length of 7 m and a mass of 2 kg pressed against it. The spring is compressed 3 m, before being released, which would project the mass away. The K of the spring is 2.43 Nm. What is the maximum velocity attained by this mass?

Note: The spring only exerts force on the mass over a length of x meters (in this case, 3 m). Mass thus reaches a maximum velocity when it has been fully propelled by the force of the entire extended spring.
