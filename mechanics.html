Mechanics basics
Newton's laws:

1. Law of inertia: An object at rest remains at rest unless affected by unbalanced force. An object in motion will remain in motion with the same speed and direction unless affected by an unbalanced force.

2. F = ma. The sum of the forces F on an object is equal to the mass m of that object multiplied by the acceleration vector a of the object.

3. When one body exerts a force on a second body, the second body simultaneously exerts a force equal in magnitude and opposite in direction on the first body.


The main formula: F=ma
Types of forces:

Force of gravity:
Definition: A force acting on all objects due to the gravitational pull of the earth.
Formula: Fg = m(g)
Gravity is a constant: -9.8m/s^2


Normal force:
Definition: A force perpendicular to the surface on which the object is placed. It only applies as much force needed to counteract gravity.
Formula: There is no constant formula attributed to this force.


Force applied:
Definition: An force applied to an object by another object.
Formula: Fa = m(a)
