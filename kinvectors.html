Kinematics
Kinematics is the study of objects in motion, without taking into account what started the motion.
Distance vs Displacement:

Distance and displacement are both measured in meters. However:

- Distance is a scalar quantity. It only has one component. Ex: 200m
- Displacement is a vector. It has a distance and a direction. Ex: 200m West

Velocity and speed:

Units of velocity and speed are in meters/second.
Speed and velocity are two different things: speed is a scalar quantity, while velocity is a vector.


Speed refers to the amount of time required to cover distance.



Velocity refers to the change in position in a certain amount of time.



Velocity takes into account the direction of the object.

Ex : Imagine someone taking a step forward, and then a step backwards, to end up at the original point. If this person repeats this motion very quickly, this person would have a high speed, but the velocity would be zero, since no change in position would occur.

The instantaneous slope of a p(t) graph describes the velocity of the object at that point.
Acceleration:

Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity at a function of time. It is a vector, and units are [m/s2]

Finding the instantaneous slope of a v(t) graph describes the acceleration of the object at that point.
